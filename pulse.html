Pulse Personal Finance and Payments

My Process

As a designer, I am passionate about creating products that solve real-world problems and improve people's lives. For this project, I focused on the issue of specialized personal finance or payment apps, which require users to have multiple accounts/apps to manage their finances. My goal was to design an app that would simplify the process and allow users to manage all their finances in one place. Through a rigorous process of research, ideation, wireframing, and prototyping, I was able to create a product that meets the needs and goals of our target users, Tommy and Lauren. In this "My Process" page, I will take you through each step of the design process and highlight the insights and solutions that led to the final product.

Research

To start this project, I identified a problem: current personal finance or payment apps are too specialized, which means a user likely has to have multiple accounts/apps in order to simply exist. Through user interviews, I was able to identify the financial needs and motivations of our target user, and use an affinity map to match the nuggets of wisdom from users with different research goals. This helped me create user personas, with Tommy as the primary persona, representing a modern millennial struggling to find financial security while also trying to enjoy life and have experiences worth posting about on Instagram. Lauren is our secondary user who already has her finances on lockdown.

Ideation

The work done at this phase will become the basis for our wireframes and eventual prototypes.

Wireframe & Lo-fi Prototype

I started by creating simple, rough designs on paper to explore multiple solutions and test ideas quickly. These wireframes helped me to identify the layout and functionality of the app. I then created a lo-fi Figma prototype that allowed me to confirm usability without getting distracted by subsequent design steps. Using this lo-fi prototype, I was able to iterate rapidly and refine the design based on user feedback.

Design

Using the wireframes and lo-fi prototypes as a foundation, I designed the final product. I focused on creating a simple and intuitive user interface that would allow users to easily navigate the app and manage their finances. I also made sure to incorporate a modern and visually appealing design to appeal to our target user's aesthetic preferences.

Prototype

With the design finalized, I created a hi-fi prototype that allowed me to continue testing the functionality with users and establish what was and wasn't working early on. Through user testing, I identified and addressed several problems and made the necessary adjustments to the app to ensure a smooth user experience. The prototype is a complete representation of the app and can be used to demonstrate its features and functionality to potential users and stakeholders.
